Who is Emily?

Whomst

Hello!! I’m Emily. I am a junior at Wheaton College in Norton, MA. I am majoring in Art History and English, entrenched in the analytical and contextual facets of my main creative outlets, visual art and creative writing. The work of female artists of the Dada and Surrealist movements like Frida Khalo, Hannah Höch, Claude Cahun and Méret Oppenheim are major inspirations for me, speaking to my fascination with gender, sexuality, and the challenge of power structures. I am interested in depictions of sexuality that deviate from the heteronormative tradition with the knowledge that the male/female relationship is the classical paradigm and the female body is historically contested as the grounds for male self discovery and fetishization. In exploring these subjects, I have used a variety of visual and linguistic mediums including but not limited to painting, drawing, collage, mixed media works, digital art, sculpture, embroidery, murals, amination, poetry, and short essays. Fashion is another medium through which I express myself. The Creative Wasteland, the business I started in 2018, expanded upon the jewelry making and clothes repurposing that I was originally just doing for myself but which now I share with those who wear my creations. In the winter of 2020, I invested in my business by participating in the social entrepreneurship bootcamp Global Center for Social Entrepreneurship Network through Wheaton. This program introduced me to the realm of digital fabrication. I have never been a technically savvy person so when I created my first earring (which I design in TinkerCad and printed using the Prusa 3D printer) I was extraordinarily excited. I am grateful for opportunities this course, Introduction to Digital Fabrication, offers for me. I aim to become fluent enough in the machines available in the Fab Lab to be able to expand my creative ideas into designs with the infinite possibilities allowed by these technologies. I am compelled by the omnipotence creative expression as a method of coping with trauma. Through my work with the Crossroads Women’s Homeless Shelter in Providence, RI, I explored the depths of this truth with the art classes I taught. My social justice work continues in my internship with Sojourner House, a domestic violence and human trafficking refuge and rehabilitation center, through outreach and through art therapeutic teaching through their Expressive Arts Program. I aim to move through the world with vibrant authenticity, playing with concepts of external representation as manifestations of my psychic landscape. You can usually find me curating Spotify playlists, reading an artist biography, wandering a thrift store, longboarding, or defacing a pair of jeans with paint doodles.
